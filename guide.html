UK Housing Association Dataset Guide

April 2025

1. Introduction

This guide presents a comprehensive simulated dataset for a UK Housing Association, tailored for analysis, predictive modeling, and strategic planning. It covers file organization, dataset descriptions, relationships, and best practices.

2. Dataset Overview

  • Properties & Assets: Physical attributes, organisational hierarchy (Estate > Block > Core), and location (Patch).
  • Tenants: Demographics, tenancy details, contact information, vulnerability indicators, and satisfaction metrics.
  • Maintenance & Repairs: Service requests, repair histories, and scheduled works.
  • Compliance: Safety inspections, certification statuses, and risk assessments.
  • Financials: Rent payments, arrears, property valuations, service charges, budgets, and performance metrics.
  • Incidents: Anti-social behaviour (ASB) logs and damp & mould reports.
  • Environment: Energy consumption and IoT sensor data.
  • Dimensions: Lookup tables for estates, blocks, cores, and patches.

3. Data Structure

Data is organized into CSV files by domain. Key categories include:

  • Property: Core housing stock details.
  • Tenant: Resident information and engagement.
  • Maintenance: Repair and maintenance records.
  • Incidents: Event and incident logs.
  • Compliance: Regulatory and safety tracking.
  • Financial: Revenue, expenses, and budget analysis.
  • Environment: Sensor and energy data streams.
  • Dimensions: Hierarchy and geographical references.

4. Key Files

  • Property Details (imputed_master_properties_enriched.csv): Comprehensive property attributes and hierarchical structure.
  • Tenant Profiles (tenants_enriched_master.csv): Demographic and satisfaction data for each tenant.
  • Maintenance Requests (maintenance_requests_master.csv): All maintenance work orders consolidated.
  • Tenant Feedback (tenant_feedback_master.csv): Satisfaction scores and comments linked to services.
  • Damp & Mould Incidents (damp_mould_incidents_master.csv): Recorded reports of damp and mould issues.
  • Compliance Records (compliance_master.csv): Inspection dates, statuses, and risk assessments.
  • Rent Payments (rent_payments_enriched_final.csv): Historical rent transactions and arrears tracking.
  • Property Valuations (financial_property_valuations.csv): Valuation trends and rental yield estimates.
  • Service Charges (financial_service_charges.csv): Quarterly service charge breakdowns by property.
  • Maintenance Budgets (financial_budgets_property.csv, financial_budgets_block.csv, financial_budgets_estate.csv): Budget versus actual spend at property, block, and estate levels.
  • Financial Performance (financial_performance_property.csv): Quarterly revenue, collection rates, void losses, and ROI metrics.

5. Relationships

Datasets are linked using these identifiers:

  • propertyid: Primary key for properties.
  • tenantid: Links tenants to feedback and payments.
  • blockid, estateid, coreid, patchid: Hierarchical and geographic references.
  • Transaction IDs: request_id, repairid, feedback_id, asb_id, compliance_id.

Refer to housing_data_dictionary_final.csv for comprehensive field definitions, data types, and example values.

7. Additional Notes

  • Simulation: Data is synthetic; distributions may not precisely match real-world scenarios.
  • Data Quality: Intentional nulls simulate real data challenges.
  • File Naming: Files ending in "_master" or "_final" are the preferred consolidated sources.
  • Date Formats: Use YYYY-MM-DD; verify consistency.
  • Data Period: Covers data up to early 2025.
